The Goal
Ready to begin Round 1 on Day 1 — with clear direction, strong conditions, prepared staff, and systems in place to support implementation and learning.
Planning for Summer Round — 4 Steps
01
Identify structures & constraints
Know what you're working with before you plan anything
▾
On- and off-contract dates for all staff
District requirements: PD days, mandatory meetings
Anticipated leadership or staffing changes
Competing priorities and shared collaboration windows
Start early. Gather this in spring — at Journey Planning Meeting 3 or in March/April — well before you need it.
02
Name the builds & decisions needed
Work backward from Round 1 to surface everything that must be ready
▾
Decisions to finalize
Remaining strategic decisions not yet resolved in Journey Planning
Decisions dependent on staffing or district finalization
Builds to create (examples)
Round 1 Plan — data plan, meeting agendas, project management
Pre-service PD and workstream training materials
Teacher-facing tools: look-fors, observation tools, surveys
Data trackers, dashboards, internal PM systems
Community communication materials
03
Prioritize builds & decisions by timing
Sequence work around staff availability, not just urgency
▾
The sequencing principle:
Decisions before people leave → Building while people are away → Refinement & launch when people return
Ask for each build or decision:
Must this be done before pre-service PD?
Must this be done before Day 1?
Can this wait until early Round 1?
Who needs to be involved — and when are they available?
04
Create the Summer Meeting Calendar & Build Plan
Turn priorities into a real plan: who does what, when
▾
Assign clear ownership to every build and decision
Schedule summer check-ins and planning team meetings
Map work across all four phases: Late Spring through Launch
Use a shared build tracker to manage the plan
This step makes the plan real. Without it, summer plans stay aspirational — and teams enter the year still getting organized instead of implementing and learning.
